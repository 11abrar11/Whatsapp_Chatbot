=== LEAD SCORING RUBRIC ===

Generate a lead score from 0-100 based on the following factors.
Evaluate ONLY what you know — do not assume or infer missing information.

SCORING FACTORS:

1. Buying Intent (0-25 points) ← MOST IMPORTANT
   - How interested is the user in actually using PP5's services?
   - 25 = strong intent (e.g., "I need a website built", "let's do this", "I want to get started", "can we set up a meeting?", asking to connect with the team, expressing urgency)
   - 18 = moderate intent (e.g., "I'm looking into options", "I might need some help", comparing providers)
   - 10 = mild intent (e.g., asking general questions about services, "what do you offer?")
   - 0 = no intent / just browsing / no buying signals

   Examples of HIGH intent signals:
   - "I need this done by next month"
   - "Can you send me a quote?"
   - "Let's discuss this further"
   - "I want to talk to your team"
   - "We're ready to start"
   - "How soon can you begin?"
   - Asking about specific deliverables, processes, or next steps

2. Business Fit (0-15 points)
   - Is their business/industry a good match for PP5's services (design, branding, digital marketing, web/app development)?
   - 15 = perfect fit (e.g., company needing branding, digital ads, website)
   - 8 = partial fit (e.g., related industry but unclear needs)
   - 0 = unknown or poor fit

3. Requirement Clarity (0-15 points)
   - Has the user clearly described what they need?
   - 15 = specific, clear requirement (e.g., "need a logo and brand identity for my restaurant chain")
   - 8 = general requirement (e.g., "need some design work")
   - 0 = no requirement stated

4. Budget (0-10 points)
   - Has the user indicated a budget?
   - 10 = realistic budget mentioned
   - 5 = vague budget indication (e.g., "we have budget allocated")
   - 0 = no budget information

5. Timeline (0-10 points)
   - Does the user have an active timeline?
   - 10 = urgent/specific timeline (e.g., "need this within 2 weeks")
   - 5 = general timeline (e.g., "sometime this quarter")
   - 0 = no timeline mentioned

6. Decision Maker (0-10 points)
   - Is the person a decision maker or influencer?
   - 10 = confirmed decision maker (e.g., "I'm the CEO" or "I make the design decisions")
   - 5 = likely influencer (e.g., marketing manager)
   - 0 = unknown role

7. Company Size (0-5 points)
   - Note: Company size alone does NOT determine lead quality. A small but interested company is still valuable.
   - 5 = medium-large company (50+ employees)
   - 3 = small company (10-50 employees)
   - 2 = micro/startup (1-10 employees)
   - 0 = unknown

8. Monthly Leads / Volume (0-10 points)
   - For clients needing ongoing work, higher volume = better fit
   - 10 = high volume needs
   - 5 = moderate needs
   - 0 = unknown or one-off project

TOTAL: Sum all factors (max 100)

CLASSIFICATION (based on score):
- 70-100 = Hot (set lead_status to "Hot")
- 40-69 = Warm (set lead_status to "Warm")
- 0-39 = Cold (set lead_status to "Cold")

IMPORTANT RULES:
- lead_status MUST ALWAYS be based on the score: Hot, Warm, or Cold
- NEVER set lead_status to "Escalated" — escalation is tracked separately via escalation_required
- If user explicitly says they're not interested → set lead_status to "Not Interested"
- If all qualification fields are filled AND score >= 50 → set lead_status to "Qualified"

CONVERSATION STAGE RULES:
- "New" = First message, no information gathered
- "Discovering" = Answering questions, starting to gather info
- "Qualifying" = Actively gathering qualification data (missing fields)
- "Qualified" = All key fields filled (business, requirement, budget, timeline, decision_maker)
- "Escalated" = Financial discussion or customer care request detected
- "Closed" = User explicitly not interested or conversation ended
